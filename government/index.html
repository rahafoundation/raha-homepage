Raha Parliament

The Raha Network will evolve over time, based on the decisions of the member-elected Raha Parliament. The Raha Parliament has two main responsibilities: changing network parameters, and approving budgets.

For instance, over time the Raha Parliament will have to lower the quantity of TBUs distributed via the Raha Basic Income to prevent the Raha Basic Income fund from running out, as there are only ten billion units. The good news is that because of Metcalfeâ€™s Law, more members means a much more valuable network. Even as the basic income decreases in amount, it will likely increase in market value.

The Raha Parliament will be elected by members of Raha, but the voting system and mechanisms are still under consideration. Help design the Raha voting system.

Parliament Budget

An example budget for how the Raha Parliament's 20% allotment from the Raha Basic Income fund might be spent is listed below. Engage on the Raha forum to advocate for your ideas for the budget, or lay the groundwork for an election campaign so you can join the Raha Parliament and approve budgets yourself!

  • 5% for expanding the network to underrepresented demographics in the Raha network.
  • 5% to expanding the Raha marketplace and establishing partnership programs to grow Raha adoption.
  • 5% Raha Network software development, maintenance, and server costs.
  • 5% towards building an endowment to help stabilize the market value of Raha.
